PickHue Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 8, 2026

PickHue does not collect, sell, rent, or transmit your personal data. There are no accounts, analytics, ads, or third-party trackers.

What is stored on your device

PickHue may store the following locally in your browser using Chrome’s chrome.storage.sync API:

  • Your theme preference (system, light, or dark)
  • Your preferred color format (HEX, RGB, HSL, or OKLCH)
  • Your recent color picks
  • Your saved color palettes (names and colors)

This data stays on your device and may sync across your Chrome profile if you have Chrome Sync enabled. PickHue has no servers and cannot access this data.

What PickHue accesses when you use it

When you click the PickHue icon or activate the eyedropper, the extension may:

  • Inject a small UI panel into the current webpage
  • Capture a screenshot of the visible tab to sample pixel colors accurately

Screenshots are processed entirely on your device to power the magnifier. They are not uploaded, stored remotely, or shared.

Permissions

PickHue requests broad host access so the eyedropper can run on the pages you choose to pick colors from. The extension only acts when you invoke it from the toolbar.

Contact

Questions? Open an issue on GitHub or visit pickhue.site.
